How Residential Solar Panels Work

Understanding the technology that powers your home with clean, renewable energy

Simple. Reliable. Efficient.

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity through photovoltaic cells. When sunlight hits these cells, they generate direct current (DC) electricity. An inverter then converts this DC power into alternating current (AC) electricity—the type used by your home appliances and lighting.

1. Solar Panels

Panels on your roof capture sunlight and convert it into DC electricity through photovoltaic cells.

2. Inverter

The inverter converts DC electricity into AC electricity that your home can use immediately.

3. Power Your Home

Clean solar electricity powers your lights, appliances, heating, and everything else in your home.

4. Grid Connection

Excess energy goes to the grid for credits, and you draw from the grid when needed at night.

Solar Incentives & Rebates in Edmonton, Alberta

Maximize your savings with federal, provincial, and municipal solar programs

Save Thousands on Your Solar Installation

Between federal grants, provincial rebates, and municipal incentives, Edmonton homeowners can reduce their solar installation costs by $5,000-$10,000 or more.

Federal Programs

  • Canada Greener Homes Grant - Up to $5,000 for solar PV installations
  • Federal Investment Tax Credit - 30% tax credit on installation costs
  • Interest-free loans - Up to $40,000 for energy efficiency improvements

Alberta Programs

  • Residential and Commercial Solar Program - Provincial rebates for installations
  • Net Metering - Earn credits for excess energy sent to the grid
  • Property Tax Exemptions - Solar equipment exempt from property tax increases
