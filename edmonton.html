Why Choose Solar in Edmonton?

Edmonton receives excellent solar radiation despite common misconceptions about Alberta's climate

Exceptional Solar Potential

Edmonton receives approximately 2,300 hours of sunshine annually—more than many traditionally "sunny" locations. The city's northern latitude provides exceptionally long summer days, with the sun setting as late as 10 PM during peak production months, maximizing daily energy generation.

Alberta's high elevation and clear, dry air allow solar panels to receive intense, direct sunlight with less atmospheric interference than coastal or humid climates. Solar panels actually perform more efficiently in Edmonton's cooler temperatures compared to hot desert climates.

Edmonton's solar irradiance (the measure of solar energy received) is comparable to Germany—a world leader in solar adoption—proving that cold climates can achieve excellent solar performance with proper system design.

Benefits of Solar in Edmonton

Unique advantages for Edmonton homeowners and businesses

High Electricity Rates

Edmonton's electricity rates are among the highest in Western Canada, with increasing volatility in the deregulated Alberta market. Solar allows you to lock in energy costs and protect against future rate increases, providing predictable long-term savings that improve ROI compared to regions with lower rates.

Net Metering Program

Alberta's net metering program allows Edmonton solar customers to receive full retail credit for excess electricity sent back to the grid. This means your solar panels continue generating value even when producing more electricity than you use, making systems financially viable for year-round optimization.

Federal & Provincial Incentives

Edmonton residents can access up to $5,000 from the Canada Greener Homes Grant, plus the federal 30% Investment Tax Credit for renewable energy systems. These incentives significantly reduce upfront costs and improve payback periods, making solar more accessible than ever before.

Increased Home Value

Edmonton's competitive real estate market values energy-efficient homes. Solar installations can increase property values by $15,000-$30,000 while making homes more attractive to environmentally conscious buyers. Solar homes sell faster and command premium prices in Edmonton's market.

Winter Performance

Despite common misconceptions, solar panels perform excellently in Edmonton winters. Snow typically slides off angled panels naturally, and the combination of cold temperatures (which increase panel efficiency) and snow reflection (which boosts light absorption) can actually improve winter production.

Environmental Impact

Alberta's electricity grid is still largely coal and natural gas powered, making solar especially impactful for reducing carbon emissions. A typical Edmonton residential system offsets 3-5 tons of CO2 annually— equivalent to planting 75-125 trees or taking a car off the road.
