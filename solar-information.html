Frequently Asked Questions

The top 5 questions we receive about solar panels and solar energy

How long do solar panels last?

Solar panels are extremely durable and typically last 25-30 years or more. Most manufacturers offer 25-year performance warranties guaranteeing at least 80-85% of original output after 25 years. The panels don't "stop working" after 25 years—they simply produce slightly less power. With proper maintenance, solar panels can continue generating electricity for 40+ years.

Do solar panels work in winter or on cloudy days?

Yes! Solar panels work in winter and on cloudy days, though at reduced capacity. They need light, not heat, to generate electricity. In fact, solar panels are more efficient in cooler temperatures. In Alberta, solar panels produce excellent energy year-round. Winter production is lower due to shorter days and lower sun angles, but annual production remains strong. Snow typically slides off angled panels or melts quickly due to the dark panel surface.

What happens to solar power when the grid goes down?

Standard grid-tied solar systems automatically shut down during power outages for safety reasons—this prevents electricity from back-feeding into the grid and potentially harming utility workers. If you want backup power during outages, you need to add battery storage to your system. With batteries, your solar panels can continue powering your home during grid outages, providing true energy independence.

How much maintenance do solar panels require?

Solar panels require minimal maintenance. Rain naturally cleans panels in most cases, though professional cleaning every 1-2 years can optimize performance. Annual inspections are recommended to check electrical connections, mounting hardware, and inverter operation. Most maintenance involves monitoring system performance through your inverter or monitoring app to ensure everything operates correctly. Snow removal is rarely needed as panels shed snow naturally.

Will solar panels damage my roof?

When installed properly by certified professionals, solar panels will not damage your roof. In fact, they can protect the covered portion from weather exposure. We use specialized mounting systems with flashing and sealants that prevent water intrusion. Professional installers assess roof condition before installation and ensure your roof can support the system weight (solar panels are surprisingly light—about 2-4 lbs per square foot). If your roof needs repairs or replacement, it's best to address that before installing solar.

Understanding Solar Costs & Return on Investment

Breaking down the economics of solar energy to help you make informed decisions

What Affects Solar System Costs?

Solar system pricing depends on several factors: system size (measured in kilowatts), equipment quality, roof complexity, and installation requirements. A typical residential system in Western Canada ranges from $15,000-$30,000 before incentives. Commercial systems range from $50,000 to several hundred thousand dollars depending on scale.

However, upfront cost is only part of the equation. Federal and provincial incentives can reduce costs by 30-50%, and financing options make solar accessible with zero down payment in many cases.

7-10 Years

Average payback period for residential solar systems in Alberta, after which you enjoy free electricity.

$30,000-$80,000

Lifetime savings for a typical residential solar system over 25-30 years of operation.

10-25% ROI

Annual return on investment for solar systems, outperforming most traditional investment vehicles.

Smart Tip: Think Long-Term

When evaluating solar costs, consider the 25-30 year lifespan rather than just upfront price. A $25,000 system that saves you $2,500 annually pays for itself in 10 years, then generates pure profit for another 15-20 years. Factor in rising electricity rates, and solar becomes one of the best investments you can make for your property.
