How long does installation typically take?

Residential installations typically take 1-3 days depending on system size and roof complexity. Commercial installations take 1-4 weeks depending on scale. The permitting process can add 2-6 weeks before installation. We handle all permitting and utility paperwork for you.

How much maintenance do solar panels require?

Solar panels require minimal maintenance. Rain naturally cleans panels in most cases. Professional cleaning every 1-2 years optimizes performance. Annual inspections check electrical connections and mounting hardware. Monitor system performance through your app to ensure optimal operation.

Will solar panels damage my roof?

When installed properly by certified professionals, solar panels will not damage your roof. They actually protect the covered area from weather. We use specialized mounting with flashing and sealants that prevent water intrusion. If your roof needs repairs, address them before solar installation.

How long do solar panels last?

Solar panels typically last 25-30 years or more. Most manufacturers offer 25-year performance warranties guaranteeing at least 80-85% of original output. Panels don't "stop" after 25 years—they just produce slightly less power. With proper maintenance, panels can generate electricity for 40+ years.

What warranty coverage do you provide?

We provide comprehensive 25-year warranties covering equipment, workmanship, and performance. Solar panels have 25-year manufacturer warranties, inverters typically 10-25 years depending on model. Our installation workmanship is guaranteed for 25 years—among the best in the industry.

How much does a solar system cost?

Residential systems in Western Canada typically range from $15,000-$30,000 before incentives, depending on system size and equipment quality. Commercial systems range from $50,000 to several hundred thousand dollars. After federal and provincial incentives, net costs are significantly lower. Financing options available.

What is the payback period for solar?

Most residential systems in Alberta have a payback period of 7-10 years. Commercial systems often see 5-8 year payback due to higher electricity rates and better tax treatment. After payback, you enjoy 15-20+ years of free electricity. ROI typically ranges from 10-25% annually.

Are there government incentives available?

Yes! Federal programs like the Canada Greener Homes Grant offer up to $5,000 for residential solar. Commercial installations can access accelerated CCA depreciation. Municipal and utility rebates vary by location. We help identify and apply for all available incentives to maximize your savings.

Will solar increase my property value?

Studies show solar panels increase property values by 3-4% on average. Homes with solar sell faster and for premium prices. Buyers value lower energy costs and environmental benefits. Commercial properties see improved valuations and lower operating costs, making them more attractive to tenants and buyers.

How much can I save on electricity bills?

Most residential customers save 50-90% on electricity bills depending on system size and usage patterns. Over 25 years, typical savings range from $30,000-$80,000. Commercial facilities often save even more due to higher consumption and time-of-use rate structures. Savings increase as electricity rates rise over time.

Do solar panels work in winter or on cloudy days?

Yes! Panels work year-round, though at reduced capacity in winter and cloudy weather. They need light, not heat. Alberta gets excellent annual solar production. Winter has lower output due to shorter days, but snow typically slides off angled panels. Solar panels are actually more efficient in cooler temperatures.

What happens during a power outage?

Standard grid-tied systems automatically shut down during outages for safety—preventing back-feed to the grid that could harm utility workers. For backup power, add battery storage to your system. With batteries, solar panels continue powering your home during outages, providing energy independence.

How much roof space do I need?

A typical residential system needs 300-600 square feet of south-facing roof space. Each solar panel is about 18 square feet. West and east-facing roofs work well too, though slightly less efficient than south. We assess your specific roof during consultation to optimize panel placement for maximum production.

Is my home suitable for solar?

Most homes are suitable! Ideal conditions include south-facing roof with minimal shade, good structural condition, and adequate space. However, we successfully install on east, west, and even north-facing roofs. Ground-mount systems work if roof isn't suitable. Free consultation includes suitability assessment.

Can I add more panels later?

Yes! Systems can be expanded as your needs grow. Consider planning for expansion during initial design—it's more cost-effective than retrofitting. Common scenarios: adding EV charging, installing heat pump, or increased consumption. Battery storage can also be added to existing systems for backup power capability.

Understanding Solar Costs & Return on Investment

Breaking down the economics of solar energy to help you make informed decisions

What Affects Solar System Costs?

Solar system pricing depends on several factors: system size (measured in kilowatts), equipment quality, roof complexity, and installation requirements. A typical residential system in Western Canada ranges from $15,000-$30,000 before incentives. Commercial systems range from $50,000 to several hundred thousand dollars depending on scale.

However, upfront cost is only part of the equation. Federal and provincial incentives can reduce costs by 30-50%, and financing options make solar accessible with zero down payment in many cases.

7-10 Years

Average payback period for residential solar systems in Alberta, after which you enjoy free electricity.

$30,000-$80,000

Lifetime savings for a typical residential solar system over 25-30 years of operation.

10-25% ROI

Annual return on investment for solar systems, outperforming most traditional investment vehicles.

Smart Tip: Think Long-Term

When evaluating solar costs, consider the 25-30 year lifespan rather than just upfront price. A $25,000 system that saves you $2,500 annually pays for itself in 10 years, then generates pure profit for another 15-20 years. Factor in rising electricity rates, and solar becomes one of the best investments you can make for your property.
